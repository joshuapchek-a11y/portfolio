PHOTOGRAPHY

(2025)

AFTER HENSON

‘After Henson’ responds to, and engages in dialogue with Bill Henson’s work Untitled Sequence (1979). Henson has established himself as one of Australia’s most significant contemporary photographers. The work is beautiful, like much of his portfolio, discussing themes of identity and adolescence, but takes agency away from his subject. This raises a major ethical question regarding the agency of his models. In this work I aim not to recreate a the Henson photograph, but to establish a dialogue between myself and my model, by photographing him in his space - and giving him the agency to markup the photograph. In doing so I attempt to shift the power back to the subject, to portray himself as he wishes.

archival inkjet print on ilford pearl, 335 x 420 mm

this work was collaborative, with the consent and active participation of the subject, Seamus Byrne
